Adrianne Curry | Modeling, TV Shows, Films, and Books

Modeling

Curry took home the title of Tyra Banks’ America’s Next Top Model, earning a contract with Wilhelmina Models in New York City and dozens of magazine modeling assignments.

Further, she modeled for magazines like Life & Style, Star, Maxim, and Spanish Marie Claire. 

Anne Bowen Spring 2005, Jaime Pressly, Pamela Anderson’s line, Ed Hardy, Von Dutch, and Christopher Deane are her runway shows.

Similarly, Curry photographed for the renowned magazine and was even featured on Playboy’s American issue cover in February 2006.

She also modeled for a technology demonstration produced by Nvidia to showcase their video cards in late 2006. Along with Lisa Rinna and Denise Richards, she was a spokesmodel for “The Flex Belt.”

TV Shows

The talented Adrianne Curry went on to star in The Surreal Life, a reality television show.

Further, she ended up filming with Christopher Knight of “The Brady Bunch,” and the two began dating soon after the show ended.

Curry and her then-boyfriend, actor Christopher Knight, were given their show, “My Fair Brady,” on VH1.

Her plastic surgery and Knight’s desire to create a family featured prominently in the show.

She also appeared in Courteney Cox’s ‘Dirt,’ where she had a scene with her.

Besides, Curry was a panelist judge on the second season of The Tester, a PlayStation Network reality show, as the “Resident Celebrity Gamer.”

She also hosted live coverage of Blizzcon 2011 for Direct TV and lived coverage of E3 2011 and 2012 for Namco Bandai.

Films

Curry has appeared in a few films. Her debut film appearance was in 2006’s “Fallen Angels.”

Similarly, she appeared in the thriller “Jack Rio” and “Light Years Away” two years later.

Curry made a cameo appearance in the horror-comedy anthology film “Tales of Halloween” in 2015. A pair of teenagers hijack her car, run down trick-or-treaters, and ransack a convenience store.
