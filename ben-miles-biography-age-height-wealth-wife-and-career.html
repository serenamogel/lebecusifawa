How did Ben Miles start his Professional Career?

Ben made his acting debut in 1989 as Barnardo in the Shakespeare Theatre production of “Hamlet” in Stratford-upon-Royal Avon. In 1993, he appeared in a production of ‘The Two Gentlemen of Verona’ by the Royal Shakespeare Company at London’s Barbican Theatre.

In 2009, he was a Tony Award-winning member of the cast of “The Norman Conquests.” He also performed in the 1995 production of Molière’s “The Miser” at the Chichester Festival Theatre in Chichester, West Sussex, directed by Ranjit Bolt.

Ben made his cinematic debut in the blockbuster comedy ‘Coupling’ in 2000 and then appeared in ‘The Forsyte Saga’ and ‘Prime Suspect’ in 2002.

He portrayed DSU Jack Haley in the 2017 drama The Last Post and in the film Collateral (2018). Miles has also acted in the 2018 films ‘Red Joan’ starring Judi Dench and ‘The Catcher Was a Spy’ with Paul Rudd.
